Components of Food

Question 01:

A substance which is essential for maintaining life and growth is called

  • a. nutrient
  • b. starch
  • c. vitamin
  • d. scurvy
Answer

Answer : a. nutrient

Question 02:

Composition of fats are

  • a. oxygen, nitrogen and hydrogen
  • b. carbon, sulphur and hydrogen
  • c. carbon, sulphur and nitrogen
  • d. carbon, hydrogen and oxygen
Answer

Answer : d. carbon, hydrogen and oxygen

Question 03:

Starch can be tested by using

  • a. iodine solution
  • b. Fehling’s solution
  • c. copper sulphate
  • d. caustic soda
Answer

Answer : a. iodine solution

Question 04:

Which of the following are ‘energy-giving foods’?

  • a. Carbohydrates
  • b. Fats
  • c. None of these
  • d. Both (a) & (b)
Answer

Answer : d. Both (a) & (b)

Question 05:

Which carbohydrate is present in sugar cane?

  • a. None of these
  • b. Simple carbohydrate
  • c. Complex carbohydrate
  • d. Both (c) & (b)
Answer

Answer : c. Simple carbohydrate

Question 06:

Which of the following nutrients helps us to fight diseases?

  • a. Carbohydrates
  • b. Proteins
  • c. Roughage
  • d. Vitamins
Answer

Answer : d. Vitamins

Question 07:

Diseases caused due to the deficiency of vitamins are called

  • a. deficiency diseases
  • b. transmitted diseases
  • c. chronic diseases
  • d. dietary diseases
Answer

Answer : a. deficiency diseases

Question 08:

All the deficiency diseases can be prevented by

  • a. taking antibiotics
  • b. cleanliness
  • c. taking balanced diet
  • d. vaccination
Answer

Answer : c. taking balanced diet

Question 09:

Deficiency of vitamin A causes

  • a. beriberi
  • b. night blindness
  • c. rickets
  • d. scurvy
Answer

Answer : b. night blindness

Question 10:

Vegetable that contains water is

  • a. brinjal
  • b. potatoes
  • c. cabbage
  • d. all of these
Answer

Answer : d. all of these
