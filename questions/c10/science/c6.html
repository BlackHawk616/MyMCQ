Life Processes

Question 01:

The contraction and expansion movement of the walls of the food pipe is called:
  • a. Translocation
  • b. Transpiration
  • c. Peristaltic movement
  • d. Digestion
Answer

Answer : c. Peristaltic movement

Question 02:

The respiratory pigment in human beings is:
  • a. haemoglobin
  • b. mitochondria
  • c. carotene
  • d. chlorophyll
Answer

Answer : a. haemoglobin

Question 03:

what is emphysema?
  • a. liver disease
  • b. lung disease
  • c. heart disease
  • d. breathing disease
Answer

Answer : b. lung disease

Question 04:

A plant gets rid of excess water through transpiration. What is the method used by plants to get rid of solid waste products?
  • a. dropping down fruits
  • b. expansion of roots in the soil
  • c. shortening of stem
  • d. shedding of yellow leaves
Answer

Answer : d. shedding of yellow leaves

Question 05:

What is the percentage of oxygen in the expired air when a person is resting?
  • a. 20%
  • b. 26%
  • c. 13%
  • d. 16%
Answer

Answer : d. 16%

Question 06:

In which mode of nutrition an organism de-rives its food from the body of another living organism without killing it?
  • a. holozonic nutrition
  • b. autotrophic nutrition
  • c. parasitic nutrition
  • d. saprotrophic nutrition
Answer

Answer : c. parasitic nutrition

Question 07:

The mode of nutrition found in fungi is:
  • a. Parasitic nutrition
  • b. Holozoic nutrition
  • c. Autotrophic nutrition
  • d. Saprotrophic nutrition
Answer

Answer : d. Saprotrophic nutrition

Question 08:

Which of the following are energy foods?
  • a. Proteins and mineral salts
  • b. Carbohydrates and fats
  • c. Water and roughage
  • d. Vitamins and minerals
Answer

Answer : b. Carbohydrates and fats

Question 09:

Which of the following events in the mouth cavity will be affected if salivary amylase is lacking in the saliva?
  • a. Starch breaking down into sugars.
  • b. Fats breaking down into fatty acids and glycerol.
  • c. Proteins breaking down into amino acids.
  • d. Absorption of vitamins.
Answer

Answer : a. Starch breaking down into sugars.
