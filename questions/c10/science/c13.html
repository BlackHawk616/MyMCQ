Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

Question 01:

For a current in a long straight solenoid N- and S-poles are created at the two ends. Among the following statements, the incorrect statement is
  • a. The pattern of the magnetic field associated with the solenoid is different from the pattern of the magnetic field around a bar magnet
  • b. The N- and S-poles exchange position when the direction of current through the solenoid is reversed
  • c. The strong magnetic field produced inside the solenoid can be used to magnetise a piece of magnetic material like soft iron, when placed inside the coil
  • d. The field lines inside the solenoid are in the form of straight lines which indicates that the magnetic field is the same at all points inside the solenoid
Answer

Answer : a. The pattern of the magnetic field associated with the solenoid is different from the pattern of the magnetic field around a bar magnet

Question 02:

Choose the incorrect statement
  • a. The right-hand thumb rule is used to find the direction of magnetic fields due to current carrying conductors
  • b. The difference between the direct and alternating currents is that the direct current always flows in one direction, whereas the alternating current reverses its direction periodically
  • c. Fleming’s right-hand rule is a simple rule to know the direction of induced current
  • d. In India, the AC changes direction after every 1/50 second
Answer

Answer : d. In India, the AC changes direction after every 1/50 second

Question 03:

When a straight conductor is carrying current:
  • a. There are no magnetic field lines
  • b. There are circular magnetic field lines around it
  • c. There are magnetic field lines parallel to the conductor
  • d. None of the above
Answer

Answer : b. There are circular magnetic field lines around it

Question 04:

The mains power supply of a house is through a 2A fuse. How many 50W bulbs can be used in this house at the normal voltage?
  • a. 7
  • b. 5
  • c. 8
  • d. 6
Answer

Answer : c. 8

Question 05:

The most important safety method used for protecting home appliances from short circuiting or overloading is
  • a. earthing
  • b. use of electric meter
  • c. use of stabilizers
  • d. use of fuse
Answer

Answer : d. use of fuse

Question 06:

Two magnetic field lines:
  • a. Intersect near north-pole or south pole
  • b. Intersect at the midpoint of the magnet
  • c. Never intersect each other
  • d. Intersect at the neutral point
Answer

Answer : c. Never intersect each other

Question 07:

The magnetic field inside a long straight solenoid carrying current:
  • a. Decrease as we move towards its end
  • b. Is the same at all points
  • c. Increase as we move towards its end
  • d. Is zero
Answer

Answer : b. Is the same at all points

Question 08:

__________ are generally made up of pure tin or an alloy of tin with other metals like copper.
  • a. Fuse wires
  • b. Electric iron
  • c. Metal wires
  • d. Circuit
Answer

Answer : a. Fuse wires

Question 09:

At the time of short circuit, the current in the circuit:
  • a. Reduces substantially
  • b. Does not change
  • c. Increases heavily
  • d. Varies continuously
Answer

Answer : c. Increases heavily

Question 10:

To convert an AC generator into DC generator
  • a. slip rings and brushes must be used
  • b. a stronger magnetic field has to be used
  • c. a rectangular wire loop has to be used
  • d. split-ring type commutator must be used
Answer

Answer : d. split-ring type commutator must be used
