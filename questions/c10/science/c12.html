Electricity

Question 01:

Electrical resistivity of a given metallic wire depends upon
  • a. its length
  • b. its shape
  • c. nature of the material
  • d. its thickness
Answer

Answer : c. nature of the material

Question 02:

The least resistance obtained by using 2 Ω, 4 Ω, 1 Ω and 100 Ω is
  • a. > 2 Ω
  • b. < 1 Ω
  • c. < 4 Ω
  • d. < 100 Ω
Answer

Answer : b. < 1 Ω

Question 03:

What is the minimum resistance which can be made using five resistors each of 1/5 Ω
  • a. 1/10 Ω
  • b. 25 Ω
  • c. 1/25 Ω
  • d. 1/5 Ω
Answer

Answer : c. 1/25 Ω

Question 04:

Work of 14 J is done to move 2 C charge between two points on a conducting wire. What is the potential difference between the two points?
  • a. 7 V
  • b. 14 V
  • c. 28 V
  • d. 3.5 V
Answer

Answer : a. 7 V

Question 05:

Which of the given is the SI Unit of Electric Current?
  • a. Ohm
  • b. Volt
  • c. Faraday
  • d. Ampere
Answer

Answer : d. Ampere

Question 06:

A fuse wire is inserted in which wire?
  • a. May be connected in any line
  • b. Live wire
  • c. In the earth wire
  • d. In the neutral wire
Answer

Answer : b. Live wire

Question 07:

If the current I through a resistor is increased by 100% (assume that temperature remains unchanged), the increase in power dissipated will be
  • a. 400 %
  • b. 100 %
  • c. 300 %
  • d. 200 %
Answer

Answer : c. 300 %

Question 08:

The resistivity does not change if
  • a. the shape of the resistor is changed
  • b. the temperature is changed
  • c. the material is changed
  • d. both material and temperature are changed
Answer

Answer : a. the shape of the resistor is changed

Question 09:

The rate of flow of an electric charge is known as :
  • a. Electric conductance
  • b. Electric current
  • c. Electric potential
  • d. None of these
Answer

Answer : b. Electric current

Question 10:

The instrument used for measuring electric current is :
  • a. Voltmeter
  • b. Potentiometer
  • c. Ammeter
  • d. Galvanometer
Answer

Answer : c. Ammeter
