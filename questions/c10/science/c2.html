Acids, Bases And Salts

Question 01:

Which of the following will turn phenolphthalein pink?
  • a. H2o
  • b. HCl
  • c. CH3COOH
  • d. NaOH
Answer

Answer : d. NaOH

Question 02:

Which acid is present in ant sting?
  • a. acetic acid
  • b. oxalic acid
  • c. methanoic acid
  • d. tartaric acid
Answer

Answer : c. methanoic acid

Question 03:

Na2CO3.10H2O is known as –
  • a. Baking soda
  • b. Baking powder
  • c. Washing soda
  • d. Bleaching powder
Answer

Answer : c. Washing soda

Question 04:

Which of the following salts does not contain water of crystallisation?
  • a. Blue vitriol
  • b. Baking soda
  • c. Washing soda
  • d. Gypsum
Answer

Answer : b. Baking soda

Question 05:

What is the chemical formula of POP (Plaster of Paris)?
  • a. CaSO4.2H2O
  • b. CaSO4.3H2O
  • c. CaSO4.1/2H2O
  • d. CaCO3.1/2H2
Answer

Answer : c. CaSO4.1/2H2O

Question 06:

At what temperature is gypsum heated to form Plaster of Paris?
  • a. 75°C
  • b. 100°C
  • c. 35°C
  • d. 65°C
Answer

Answer : b. 100°C

Question 07:

When hydrogen chloride gas is prepared on a humid day, the gas is usually passed through the guard tube containing calcium chloride. The role of calcium chloride taken in the guard tube is to
  • a. absorb the evolved gas
  • b. moisten the gas
  • c. absorb Cl– ions from the evolved gas
  • d. absorb moisture from the gas
Answer

Answer : d. absorb moisture from the gas

Question 08:

In terms of acidic strength, which one of the following is in the correct increasing order?
  • a. Water < Hydrochloric acid < Acetic acid
  • b. Water < Acetic acid < Hydrochloric acid
  • c. Acetic acid < Water < Hydrochloric acid
  • d. Hydrochloric acid < Water < Acetic acid
Answer

Answer : b. Water < Acetic acid < Hydrochloric acid

Question 09:

Brine is an
  • a. aqueous solution of sodium hydroxide
  • b. aqueous solution of sodium carbonate
  • c. aqueous solution of sodium chloride
  • d. aqueous solution of sodium bicarbonate
Answer

Answer : c. aqueous solution of sodium chloride

Question 10:

Alkalis are
  • a. bases, which are soluble in water
  • b. acids, which are insoluble in water
  • c. bases, which are insoluble in water
  • d. acids, which are soluble in water
Answer

Answer : a. bases, which are soluble in water
