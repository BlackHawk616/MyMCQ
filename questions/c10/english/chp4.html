From the Diary of Anne Frank

Question 01:

Extract

“Mr Keesing had a good laugh at my arguments, but when I proceeded to talk my way through the next lesson, he assigned me a second essay. This time it was supposed to be on ‘An Incorrigible Chatterbox’. I handed it in, and Mr Keesing had nothing to complain about for two whole lessons. However, during the third lesson, he’d finally had enough. “Anne Frank, as a punishment for talking in class, wrote an essay entitled-‘Quack, Quack, Quack, said Mistress Chatterbox’.”

(I) What convincing argument was made by Anne?
  • a. She found it impossible to be quiet like the others as she couldn’t change herself.
  • b. She had inherited the trait from her mother, so she couldn’t stop being talkative.
  • c. She had the right to be talkative, as it was a classroom and not a prison.
  • d. She was talkative, just like any other student in the class.
Answer

Answer : b. She had inherited the trait from her mother, so she couldn’t stop being talkative.

(II) Based on this extract, pick the option with the list of words that best describe Mr Keesing.
  • a. innovative and jovial
  • b. tolerant and strict
  • c. strict and innovative
  • d. jovial and creative
Answer

Answer : c. strict and innovative

(III) How did Anne feel when she was punished the third time by Mr Keesing? She
  • a. was worried as she had run out of original ideas for her essay.
  • b. was happy as she had to write three essays on the same topic.
  • c. enjoyed making fun of Mr Keesing in her own way.
  • d. was thrilled at another opportunity to showcase her writing abilities.
Answer

Answer : a. was worried as she had run out of original ideas for her essay.

(IV) What does ‘had a good laugh’ imply in the context of Mr Keesing? It means that he
  • a. ridiculed Anne in front of the whole class.
  • b. pulled up Anne for her arguments in the essay.
  • c. realised the humour in it and was amused.
  • d. celebrated his ability to make Anne write the essay.
Answer

Answer : c. realised the humour in it and was amused.

(V) What does ‘had a good laugh’ imply in the context of Mr Keesing? It means that he
  • a. her to apologise and not repeat her talkative behaviour.
  • b. Anne to express her inability to elaborate on such a topic.
  • c. Anne to explore her creative writing skills.
  • d. that this would embarrass Anne and would check her indiscipline
Answer

Answer : d. that this would embarrass Anne and would check her indiscipline

Question 02:

Anne doesn’t plan to let anyone else read her diary as
  • a. she wants to cherish these moments herself.
  • b. it’s about her intimate feelings.
  • c. she is secretive about her life.
  • d. she is unwilling to share it with anyone.
Answer

Answer : b. it’s about her intimate feelings.
