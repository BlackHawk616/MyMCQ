The Hundred Dressess - 1

Question 01:

Extract-1

“In the opinion of the judges, any one of the drawings is worthy of winning the prize. I am very happy to say that Wanda Petronski is the winner of the girls’ medal. Unfortunately, Wanda has been absent from school for some days and is not here to receive the applause that is due to her. Let us hope she will be back tomorrow. Now, class, you may file around the room quietly and look at her exquisite drawings.”

(I) Pick the statement that is TRUE, according to the information given in the extract.
  • a. Wanda would have still won the prize even if she had submitted just one entry.
  • b. Wanda would have still won the prize if she had drawn something else.
  • c. Wanda won the prize because she had submitted a variety of entries.
  • d. Wanda won the prize because the majority of judges were women.
Answer

Answer : a. Wanda would have still won the prize even if she had submitted just one entry.

(II) The teacher refers to Wanda’s designs as ‘exquisite’ because
  • a. each one had the same colour theme as the other.
  • b. each of them was a copy of the latest fashion trend.
  • c. each one of them was very beautiful.
  • d. each one of the hundred designs was different.
Answer

Answer : c. each one of them was very beautiful.

(III) When the teacher wants them to ‘file around’, she wants the students to
  • a. gather around her table to discuss the designs.
  • b. walk in a line to admire the designs.
  • c. put the files in their proper places.
  • d. file the designs properly in their folders.
Answer

Answer : b. walk in a line to admire the designs.

(IV) Miss Mason says, “I am very happy to …”. Which phrase DOES NOT replace this phrase correctly from those given below?
  • a. I am sure you’ll be surprised to…
  • b. It gives me great pleasure to…
  • c. I am delighted to…
  • d. It fills me with joy to…
Answer

Answer : a. I am sure you’ll be surprised to…

(V) Pick the sentence that DOES NOT use ‘due to’ in the same sense as in the given extract.
  • a. I have some extra money due to me this month from my friend.
  • b. You have an apology due to your parents.
  • c. She was disappointed due to their rude behaviour.
  • d. We must give our colleagues vacation due to them.
Answer

Answer : c. She was disappointed due to their rude behaviour.

Question 02:

Extract-2

She always wore a faded blue dress that didn’t hang right. It was clean, but it looked as though it had never been ironed properly. She didn’t have any friends, but a lot of girls talked to her. Sometimes, they surrounded her in the schoolyard as she stood watching the little girls play hopscotch on the worn hard ground. “Wanda,’’ Peggy would say in a most courteous manner, as though she were talking to Miss Mason. “Wanda,” she’d say, giving one of her friends a nudge, “tell us. How many dresses did you say you had hanging up in your closet?”

(I) Regarding Wanda being teased, Miss Mason was
  • a. unaware that the students were frequently teasing Wanda.
  • b. in denial that such behaviour was possible by her students.
  • c. aware but didn’t want to discuss it with the students.
  • d. of the belief that such behaviour was a normal part of growing up.
Answer

Answer : a. unaware that the students were frequently teasing Wanda.

(II) Why was Wanda’s answer to Peggy’s question always the same? Pick the LEAST probable reason.

This was so because she knew that

  • a. sticking to an unexpected answer would get her the attention she needed.
  • b. confessing the truth would probably worsen the situation.
  • c. she was being picked on, and it was probably her defence.
  • d. she didn’t know how else to get out of that situation.
Answer

Answer : a. sticking to an unexpected answer would get her the attention she needed.

(III) Peggy gave her friend a nudge because
  • a. she was teasing Wanda and wanted her to make others pay attention.
  • b. she was teasing Wanda and didn’t want anyone to miss the ‘fun’.
  • c. she disliked her friend being distracted then.
  • d. she wanted to push her away from the scene.
Answer

Answer : b. she was teasing Wanda and didn’t want anyone to miss the ‘fun’.

(IV) The ‘most courteous manner’ here means that Peggy was
  • a. respectful to Wanda.
  • b. on her best behaviour.
  • c. teasing Wanda.
  • d. trying to impress Wanda.
Answer

Answer : c. teasing Wanda.

(V) Pick the option having the words that DO NOT loosely match the word ‘closet’ from those given.

1) wardrobe, 2) loft, 3) cabinet, 4) cupboard, 5) porch, 6) cellar

  • a. 2, 4 and 1
  • b. 1, 3 and 2
  • c. 3, 1 and 5
  • d. 1, 3 and 4
Answer

Answer : d. 1, 3 and 4
