Nelson Mandela - Long Walk to Freedom

Question 01:

Extract

“We, who were outlaws not so long ago, have today been given the rare privilege to be host to the nations of the world on our own soil. We thank all of our distinguished international guests for having come to take possession with the people of our country of what is, after all, a common victory for justice, for peace, for human dignity.”

I) The guests at the spectacular ceremony are called distinguished because
  • a. they are eminent world leaders witnessing it.
  • b. they are visiting the country for this purpose.
  • c. they have resumed diplomatic relations with the country
  • d. they have been invited as guests to attend it.

    Answer

    Answer : a. they are eminent world leaders witnessing it.

II) Why does the speaker say that it is a ‘rare privilege’? He says this as they have
  • a. been deprived of this honour.
  • b. experienced it for the first time.
  • c. been chosen over other countries for this honour
  • d. seldom been given this honour.
Answer

Answer: b. experienced it for the first time.

III) It is a victory for ‘human dignity’. Pick the option that lists the correct answer for what ‘human dignity’ would include.
  • a. i) immorality ii) self-respect iii) equality
  • b. i) equality ii) liberty iii) indecency
  • c. i) liberty ii) indecency iii) self-respect
  • d. i) equality ii) liberty iii) self-respect
Answer

Answer: d. i) equality ii) liberty iii) self-respect.

IV) Pick the option that showcases the usage of ‘host’ as in the extract.
  • a. She was able to host the event without any hindrance.
  • b. He was praised for his hospitality as the host of the party.
  • c. He is the best host that one can ever come across.
  • d. She met the host and apologised for her friend’s misbehaviour.
Answer

Answer: a. She was able to host the event without any hindrance.

Question 02:

Extract

“It was only when I began to learn that my boyhood freedom was an illusion when I discovered as a young man that my freedom had already been taken from me, that I began to hunger for it. At first, as a student, I wanted freedom only for myself, the transitory freedom of being able to stay out at night, read what I pleased and go where I chose. Later, as a young man in Johannesburg, I yearned for the basic and honourable freedoms…”

I) Choose the option that best fits the usage of the word ‘illusion’ as used in the extract.
  • a. I was living under the illusion that this was possible.
  • b. He was never able to get past the illusion.
  • c. A large mirror in the room creates an illusion.
  • d. The illusion I experienced was quite intriguing.

    Answer

    Answer : a. I was living under the illusion that this was possible.

II) The title that best suits this extract is
  • a. Significance of Freedom
  • b. Realisation of Freedom
  • c. Knowledge about Freedom
  • d. Freedom for Everything

    Answer

    Answer : a. Significance of Freedom

III) Why do you think the speaker mentions some freedoms as ‘transitory’?
  • a. Freedom means different things to different people.
  • b. Freedom means different things to different people.
  • c. The definition of freedom is constant, but perspectives differ.
  • d. The freedoms are momentary and keep changing with time.

    Answer

    Answer : d. The freedoms are momentary and keep changing with time.

IV) A part of the extract has been paraphrased. Choose the option that includes the most appropriate solution to the blanks in the given paraphrase of the extract.

The speaker’s belief about freedom since childhood proved false. It was not until the speaker grew up to be a young man when it i) __________________ on him that he was ii) ________________ of freedom. Then he began iii) __________________ it.

  • a. i) desired ii) dawned iii) depriving
  • b. i) dawned ii) arrived iii) desiring
  • c. i) arrived ii) deprived iii) dawned
  • d. i) dawned ii) deprived iii) desiring

    Answer

    Answer : d. i) dawned ii) deprived iii) desiring
