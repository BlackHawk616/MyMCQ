Fire and Ice

Question 01:

The poet says that he has tasted:
  • a. desire
  • b. hatred
  • c. joy
  • d. memory
Answer

Answer : a. desire

Question 02:

What does the poet compare fire with?
  • a. Hot
  • b. Hatred
  • c. Desire
  • d. Both a. and b.
Answer

Answer : c. Desire

Question 03:

What would suffice if the world were to perish twice?
  • a. Fire
  • b. Ice
  • c. Both a. and b.
  • d. Hatred
Answer

Answer : c. Both a. and b.

Question 04:

What is the rhyming schemeof the poem?
  • a. abab bcbcb
  • b. abaa bcbcb
  • c. aaab bcbcb
  • d. aaba bcbcb
Answer

Answer : b. abaa bcbcb

Question 05:

What would be a better option to end the earth?
  • a. Ice
  • b. Fire
  • c. None of the above
  • d. Both are equally competent (a) & (b)
Answer

Answer : d. Both are equally competent (a) & (b)

Question 06:

Name the poetic device used in the line “To say that for destruction ice is also great”.
  • a. Oxymoron
  • b. Imagery
  • c. Metaphor
  • d. Alliteration
Answer

Answer : b. Imagery

Question 07:

Where has he used personification?
  • a. Ice
  • b. Fire
  • c. Both a. and b.
  • d. Earth
Answer

Answer : c. Both a. and b

Question 08:

The world is full of desire and:
  • a. passion
  • b. hatred
  • c. love
  • d. hope

Answer : b. hatred

Question 09:
